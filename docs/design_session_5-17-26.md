## Decisions
- It is a roguelike
- There will be a biome-esque system based around the buildings and areas for the fighting
- Each cycle will be a new city, and the city layout will be dynamically generated - what buildings there are, the order, etc.
- Current working title of our project is 'Rewilding'
- It is a game about a newly born plant trying to reclaim the environment left behind after a highly industrialized society has disappeared

## Notes
- Do we want to be a plant morphing and growing, or some sort of druidic/natural being developing a plant army?
- We think it's better for you to be some natural force wielding and modifying plant creatures to overtake
- This being could be made of the plants - they're attached in some way, sort of like a Poison Ivy-esque situation
- An elemental being - the embodiment of the earth. He is more like a pilot or a master than the actual thing doing the attacks

## Ideas
- You start out in a little abandoned potting shed and you have to defeat the items in there - nails and screws, dust bunnies, peg board hooks, batteries, etc.
- You have supportive items at your disposal - a little bit of fertilizer, bird seeds, etc.
- Japanese-style houses have Bonsai's
- You can have card classes - Bonsai's, Vines, Moss, Flowers, Bushes, Trees, Grass
- Planting seeds for future battles
- Difficulty system based on setting - rural vs suburban vs city


- You're a soil-based creature who has received a spark of life somehow
- You have to start building up strength and developing an army of plants to take the environment back
